Official Competition Rules

WCA Regulations ↗
We will follow the WCA Regulations as much as possible for all events, while adapting where needed for an online format.

1) Eligibility

  • Participants must register using their real name and valid contact details.
  • One participant = one registration. Duplicate entries may be removed.

2) Competition Login Credentials (Mandatory)

Important: Each competition uses official Cubeology competition credentials. These credentials are compulsory for participation and verification.
  • Credential Delivery:
    The Login ID and Password for the specific competition will be sent to the same email address used during registration/payment. Participants must ensure this email is correct and accessible.
  • One Credential Per Participant:
    Each registered participant receives one unique Login ID and Password for that competition.
  • Same Credentials for All Events:
    The same Login ID and Password must be used for all events that the participant is registered for in that competition. You do not receive different logins for different events.
  • Compulsory Usage:
    These credentials are mandatory to:
    • Access the competition timer/system
    • Perform and record solves
    • Submit times on the platform
    • Submit video links for verification (per event)
    Solves performed outside the official logged-in system may be considered invalid.
  • Same ID/Password Required for Video Submission (Per Event):
    The same Login ID used to compete must also be associated with your video submission for each event. Organizers may cross-check login data with submitted videos for verification.
  • No Sharing of Credentials:
    Login credentials are strictly personal. Sharing, transferring, or using another participant’s credentials may lead to:
    • Invalidation of solves
    • Disqualification from the event/competition
  • Loss of Credentials:
    If you do not receive or lose your credentials, you must contact organizers before the competition starts. Delays caused by missing login access are the participant’s responsibility.

3) Events & Format (Ao5)

  • Format for each event is Ao5 (Average of 5).
  • You will perform 5 solves per event.
  • The average is computed according to standard cubing practice (fastest and slowest removed, average of remaining 3).
  • Rankings are generated automatically by the platform.

4) Scrambles

  • Scrambles are generated and displayed by the competition system.
  • Each scramble must be used only once for its corresponding attempt.
  • Using external scrambles or repeating a scramble is not allowed and may result in DNF for that attempt/event.

5) Inspection Time

  • A maximum of 15 seconds inspection time is allowed before each solve.
  • Inspection violations may result in penalties as per standard cubing practice:
    • +2 seconds for minor inspection overrun
    • DNF for excessive inspection violation

6) Timer Mode (Recommended)

  • Use the official Cubeology competition timer to record solves.
  • The timer captures time automatically; manually editing timer-recorded times is not allowed.
  • Any abnormal pattern of submissions may be reviewed.

7) Typing Mode (Manual Entry)

  • Times must be entered in valid cubing formats, for example:
    • 12.34
    • 1:05.21
  • Invalid formats or suspicious entries may be rejected or flagged for verification.

8) Event-Specific Rules

  • 3×3, 2×2, Pyraminx, Skewb, Megaminx and similar twisty puzzles:
    Standard solving procedures apply. The puzzle must be solved correctly as per official cubing standards.
  • Clock Event:
    • After completing the solve, both sides of the clock must be clearly shown on video.
    • If video is required for the competition/event, the “both sides shown” requirement is mandatory.
    • Failure to show both sides after the solve may result in DNF for that attempt/event.

9) Video Recording (Mandatory)

Mandatory Rule: Video recording is compulsory for all participants for all events and all solves. Any solve submitted without a valid video may be treated as DNF or may be rejected.
  • Recording must start before your attempt begins and must continue until the platform shows that your solves are submitted/recorded. This means the video should cover the full flow from login / opening the timer page to the final “all solves submitted” screen.
  • The video must clearly show ALL of the following at the same time:
    • Your cube (the puzzle must be visible throughout the solve)
    • Your hands during inspection and solving
    • Your timing method (physical timer/phone timer, if used) OR clearly show the on-screen timer if you are using the website timer
    • The Cubeology website screen showing the scramble/time entry and the recording/submission on the platform
  • Scramble visibility is compulsory: The scrambled state must be shown clearly before starting each solve. You must not hide the scramble view or switch camera angles in a way that breaks visibility.
  • Clock Event (if applicable): After the solve, both sides of the clock must be shown clearly before moving to the next attempt.
  • No cuts / edits: No cuts, jumps, trims, speed changes, overlays, or transitions are allowed within an attempt. If you record the full session, it must remain continuous without any missing segments during solves.
  • Camera placement: Use a stable camera angle where cube + screen + timing device remain visible. Blurry, obstructed, or incomplete videos may be rejected.

10) Video Submission (Timed Window)

  • Submission link activation: The video submission link will become available only after the round ends.
  • Submission deadline: Once activated, you must submit your video link by midnight (12:00 AM IST) of the same day the round ends. Late submissions may not be accepted.
  • How to submit: Upload your video to a shareable platform (Google Drive / YouTube Unlisted / Dropbox, etc.) and submit the link.
  • Link access requirements (strict):
    • The link must be viewable immediately (no “request access” or pending approvals).
    • The video must not be deleted, made private, or restricted until results are finalized.
    • Ensure audio/video is intact and the full session is included.
  • If you do not submit video within the window: The organizers may, at their discretion:
    • Mark specific solves as DNF, or
    • Invalidate the event submission, or
    • Disqualify the entry in severe/repeated cases.
  • Verification: The organizing team reserves the right to request clarifications or additional proof if the video is unclear or suspicious. Final decision remains with the organizers.

11) Penalties

  • +2 seconds may be applied for minor violations such as slight misalignment or procedural mistakes.
  • DNF may be applied for:
    • Incorrect scramble usage
    • Major inspection violations
    • Invalid solve or platform misuse
    • Failure to comply with required verification/video rules

12) Fair Play & Anti-Cheating

  • All solves must be performed fairly without external assistance.
  • Any suspicious activity may be reviewed and may lead to solve invalidation or disqualification.

13) Rankings & Results

  • Results are calculated automatically by the platform.
  • Provisional rankings may change after verification checks.
  • Final rankings published by the organizers are binding.

14) Organizer’s Authority

  • The organizing team reserves the right to modify rules where necessary for fairness or platform constraints.
  • All decisions taken by the organizers are final.
